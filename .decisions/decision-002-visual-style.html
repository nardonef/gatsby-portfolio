Decision 2 of 5

Visual Style

Visual

Now that we're using WebGL shaders, we can achieve virtually any visual effect. This decision is about the personality of the animation — what it looks like, what it evokes. The blobs sit behind your name and title on a dark background, so the style needs to complement white text without overwhelming it. Think of this as the "mood" of your homepage.

Option A
Chosen

Liquid Lava

Frank Nardone
Designer and Developer
Hot, viscous blobs that look like molten lava floating in space. Deep reds, oranges, and magentas with bright cores that glow from within. The noise distortion makes edges look thick and heavy — like actual liquid with surface tension. High contrast, high energy. This says "bold and passionate" about your work.

Works well when

  • Immediate visual punch — grabs attention fast
  • Warm colors feel energetic and creative
  • High contrast against dark background

Watch out for

  • Warm palette can feel aggressive for a portfolio
  • May compete with content for attention

Option B
Recommended Chosen

Bioluminescent Deep

Frank Nardone
Designer and Developer
Think deep-sea bioluminescence. Teals, cyans, and electric purples that glow softly against the dark, like jellyfish drifting in the abyss. The blobs have bright cores that pulse and fade, with soft edges that bleed into the background. This evolves your current green palette into something more otherworldly. It's mesmerizing without being loud — visitors will stare at it. I recommend this because it's the best balance of wow factor and sophistication for a design/dev portfolio.

Works well when

  • Evolves your current green palette naturally
  • Mesmerizing without competing with content
  • Cool tones feel sophisticated and technical
  • Glow effects are trivial in GLSL

Watch out for

  • Cool palettes can feel "cold" to some visitors
  • Needs careful brightness to not wash out text

Option C
Chosen

Aurora Silk

Frank Nardone
Designer and Developer
Soft, wide bands of color that flow and overlap like the northern lights — or like colored silk floating in water. Uses the full rainbow but keeps it muted with heavy blur and low opacity, so it reads as elegant rather than chaotic. The colors slowly shift over time, so the animation never looks the same twice. This is the most "artistic" option — it says "I have taste" more than "I have technical skill."

Works well when

  • Full color range — always visually interesting
  • Soft and elegant, never aggressive
  • Slow evolution keeps it fresh on repeat visits

Watch out for

  • Multi-color can look unfocused without careful tuning
  • Heavy blur means less defined blob shapes

Option D
Chosen

Neon Plasma

Frank Nardone
Designer and Developer
High-contrast, neon-bright blobs with sharp edges and intense glow halos — like plasma orbs floating in a void. Electric green, cyan, and magenta with hard outer glow. The edges are crisp, not blurry, which makes the metaball merge/split effect really pop. This is the most "tech" feeling option — cyberpunk energy. It screams developer. Bold choice that some will love and some will find too intense.

Works well when

  • Maximum wow factor — impossible to ignore
  • Sharp edges make blob physics very visible
  • Strong "developer" / technical identity

Watch out for

  • Polarizing — too intense for some visitors
  • Can feel like a gaming site, not a portfolio
  • Bright neons can cause eye strain on long visits
